Famous Chess Players

Bobby Fischer

1943-2008

Robert James "Bobby" Fischer was an American chess grandmaster and the 11th World Chess Champion. He is widely considered one of the greatest chess players of all time. Fischer was known for his aggressive and tactical style of play, and he was particularly skilled at the endgame. In 1972, he defeated the Soviet grandmaster Boris Spassky in the World Chess Championship, becoming the first American to win the title. Fischer's victory was seen as a major Cold War victory for the United States, and it sparked a renewed interest in chess around the world.


Garry Kasparov

1963-

Garry Kimovich Kasparov is a Russian chess grandmaster and former World Chess Champion. He is widely considered one of the greatest chess players of all time, and he has been the highest-rated player in the world for much of his career. Kasparov is known for his aggressive and tactical style of play, and he has won numerous tournaments throughout his career. In 1985, at the age of 22, he became the youngest World Chess Champion in history, and he held the title until 2000.

Hikaru Nakamura

1987-

Hikaru Nakamura (1987- ) is an American chess grandmaster and five-time United States Chess Champion. He is considered one of the strongest chess players in the United States and one of the strongest American chess players of all time. He is known for his aggressive and unorthodox playing style and has consistently been ranked among the top players in the world. He became a Grandmaster at the age of 15, making him the youngest American to ever achieve this feat.
